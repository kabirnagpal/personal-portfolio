Communication is an integral part of our daily lives. People with speech or hearing impairments often feel excluded. While Automatic Speech Recognition Systems have advanced, Signed Language Recognition Systems are still in their infancy. Our research proposes an ensembled deep learning architecture for classifying sign language characters, utilizing InceptionV3 and ResNet101 to achieve 97.24% accuracy on the ASL dataset.

With the growing complexity of cyber threats, our study explores the effectiveness of supervised machine learning techniques in intrusion detection systems. By utilizing the UNSW-NB15 dataset, we compared various ML models and achieved an accuracy of 88.7%, an F-score of 88.3%, and a false alarm rate of 0.014.

We introduce a novel deep learning model for classifying environmental sounds using the ESC-50 and Urban Sound datasets. Our CNN-based architecture, leveraging ResNet-type models, achieved 89.5% validation accuracy on environmental sounds and 96.76% accuracy on urban sound classification.
